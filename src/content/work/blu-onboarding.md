## The problem

blu is a branchless bank, which means onboarding *is* the branch. Yet our funnel told a
bad story: a third of new users abandoned during identity verification. The flow asked
for the same information twice, camera guidance for the ID photo failed in low light,
and the liveness check felt like an interrogation. For a product whose promise is
"banking without the queue", the first ten minutes felt like one.

## The process

I led three squads through a full rebuild of the funnel. We started with session
recordings and a diary study of 24 first-time users across Jakarta, Medan, and Makassar
— half of them opening their first bank account ever. The findings reshaped the flow:
we moved KYC to the end (let people explore first, commit later), rewrote every
instruction at a sixth-grade reading level in Bahasa Indonesia, and redesigned the ID
capture screen around a live edge-detection frame instead of text instructions.

As lead, my job was less pushing pixels and more holding the quality bar: weekly
design critiques, a shared Figma spec kit for the three squads, and a deal with
engineering that no KYC screen ships without passing our low-light camera test.

## The outcome

KYC completion rose 27% within two quarters and overall onboarding drop-off fell by a
third. Median time from install to funded account dropped from two days to 31 minutes.
The instruction-writing guidelines we created became the bank's tone-of-voice standard
for all product copy.
