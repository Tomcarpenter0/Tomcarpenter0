## The problem

Tokopedia's mission is economic democratization, but our own funnel disagreed: most
new sellers who registered never listed a single product. The onboarding assumed a
desktop, a product catalog, and comfort with forms. Our actual next million sellers
were warung owners and home bakers with a mid-range Android phone and patchy data.

## The process

I spent six weeks doing field research with the research team across Jakarta, Surabaya,
Medan, Bandung, Semarang, and Makassar — sitting with sellers as they tried to open a
store. The pattern was clear: they didn't fail at *wanting* to sell, they failed at
product photography, pricing, and shipping setup. I redesigned onboarding as a
checklist that could be finished out of order, made the product listing camera-first
(shoot, crop, auto-brighten), and replaced the shipping form with three presets based
on what similar stores chose.

Every screen went through weekly guerrilla testing at Pasar Tanah Abang before it
shipped.

## The outcome

Seller activation — registered sellers who publish their first listing — rose 34%.
Median time from registration to first listing collapsed from three days to about 40
minutes. The camera-first listing flow became the default for all seller tiers.
